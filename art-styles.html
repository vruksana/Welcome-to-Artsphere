Art Styles and Movements

Introduction to Art Styles

Art has evolved through centuries, with various styles and movements influencing creativity across cultures. Below are some of the most notable art styles and their characteristics.

Impressionism

Impressionism began in France in the late 19th century. Artists such as Claude Monet focused on capturing light and color in their works, using quick brushstrokes and open compositions.

Surrealism

Surrealism, a 20th-century movement, sought to express the workings of the unconscious mind. Famous artists like Salvador Dalí and René Magritte created dreamlike, often bizarre compositions.

Abstract Art

Abstract art, which became prominent in the early 20th century, focuses on shapes, colors, and forms rather than representing objects realistically. Wassily Kandinsky is considered a pioneer of abstract art.
